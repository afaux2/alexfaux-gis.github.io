Relationship Between Roadway Density and NO2 Air Concentrations in LA

This was my final project for my Urban Applications of GIS class. Below, is an overview of how I conducted my GIS analysis on the relationship between roadway density and NO2 air concentrations using ArcGIS Pro.

I obtained a shapefile of all roadways in LA County from the US Census Bureau and used the Line Density tool to create a roadway density raster.

Next, I used the Zonal Statistics as Table tool to calculate averahe roadway density per zipcode in LA County.

Shows average roadway density per zipcode in LA County

The highest roadway density is in the urban center and becomes less concentrated moving outward.

Shows average NO2 levels per zipcode in LA County

The highest concentration of NO2 is in the urban center and becomes less concentrated moving outward..

Running the OLS regression showed an adjusted R2 of 0.5712, AIC of 1187.8627, and probability of 0.000000*.

Running a GWR yielded a higher adjusted R2 of 0.7460 however, it also had a higher AICc was 7706.9874.

From the scatter plot we can see that in some areas there are groups of zipcodes that have values for both average rodway density and average NO2 air concentrations that are very similar to one another. This is likely the reason that there is still clustering on the GWR.
